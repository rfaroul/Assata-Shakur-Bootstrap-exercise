Assata Shakur

"It is our duty to fight.
It is our duty to win.
We must love each other and protect each other.
We have nothing to lose but our chains."

Shakur in Cuba, date unknown
  • July 16, 1947 - Born Joanne Deborah Byron in Jamaica, Queens, NYC
  • mid-1960s - She attend Borough of Manhattan Community College (BMCC) and then the City College of New York (CCNY) where she was involved in many political activities, protests, and sit-ins
  • 1970 - Shakur becomes involved in the Black Panther Party, eventually becoming a leader of the Harlem branch
  • 1971-1973 - Shakur was charged with several crimes that she didn't commit
  • May 1973 - Shakur was involved in a shootout with police and accused of killing New Jersey State police officer Werner Foester
  • 1973-1977 - Shakur was indicted in relation to six other incidents, resulting in three acquittals and three dismissals
  • 1977 - She was convicted of the first-degree murder of Foester and seven other felonies related to the shootout
  • 1979 - Shakur escapes from prison with the help of unknown parties
  • 1984 - She flees to Cuba, where she receives political asylum, after living as a fugitive for a few years
  • May 2, 2005 - the FBI classifies Shakur as a domestic terrorist and offers a $1 million reward for assistance in her capture
  • May 2, 2013 - the FBI adds her to the Most Wanted List; she is the first woman to be listed

"The struggle to keep Assata —and Cuba — out of the clutches of North American “justice” remains a key element in the battle for Black Liberation."

Against the Current, 1999
